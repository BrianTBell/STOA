Just some papers I'm using to test phase one of the project:

1. Introduction to Reinforcement Learning (2024)
Beginner-focused overview of states, actions, policies, rewards, and algorithm categories. Also points you to books and courses.
https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.07712

2. A Tutorial Introduction to Reinforcement Learning (2023)
Covers MDPs, Q-learning, and Temporal Difference learning with a tutorial-style structure.
https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.00803

3. A Brief Survey of Deep Reinforcement Learning (2017)
Bridges basic RL into deep RL — covers DQN, policy gradient, and actor-critic methods. Highly cited and readable.
https://arxiv.org/abs/1708.05866

4. A Comprehensive Survey of RL: From Algorithms to Practical Challenges (2024)
Goes from tabular methods up through deep RL, and addresses real issues like exploration-exploitation and convergence.
https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.18892

5. Reinforcement Learning: A Survey — Kaelbling, Littman, Moore (1996)
A classic foundational paper. Still one of the best explanations of the core RL problem framing from first principles.
https://arxiv.org/pdf/cs/9605103
